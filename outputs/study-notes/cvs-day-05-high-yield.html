Angina → ACS → MI → complications · Print and stick on your wall

Atherogenesis cellular cast 9 steps

  • Step 1 — endothelial injury (HTN, dyslipidemia, smoking, hyperglycemia) → ↑ permeability + ↑ adhesion molecules.
  • Step 2 — LDL enters intima → oxidized by ROS.
  • Step 3 — monocytes adhere, migrate, become macrophages.
  • Step 4 — foam cells form via SR-A/CD36 uptake of oxLDL (NOT downregulated by intracellular cholesterol).
  • Step 5 — T cells recruited, secrete IFN-γ, sustain inflammation.
  • Step 6 — fatty streak (yellow intimal patch, age 10–20, reversible).
  • Step 7 — platelets adhere to dysfunctional endothelium → release PDGF.
  • Step 8 — smooth muscle cells migrate from media → intima → proliferate + lay down collagen → fibrous cap.
  • Step 9 — mature plaque: stable (slow growth, collaterals develop) OR vulnerable (macrophages release MMPs → rupture cap → thrombosis → ACS).

Stable angina fixed stenosis

  • Pathology: fixed atherosclerotic stenosis ≥ 70% lumen, NO thrombus.
  • Exertional, reproducible, relieved by rest or sublingual NTG.
  • ECG: normal at rest; ST↓ on stress.
  • Symptom threshold = ~70% stenosis (collaterals preserve resting flow).
  • Treatment: lifestyle + β-blocker + statin + aspirin + sublingual NTG.
  • Revascularization: PCI or CABG depending on anatomy + LV function.

Stress testing modality choice

  • Exercise ECG: if patient can exercise + baseline ECG normal.
  • Stress echo: baseline ECG abnormal.
  • Dobutamine stress echo: can't exercise (β1 agonist mimics exercise).
  • Adenosine/regadenoson nuclear: can't exercise; "coronary steal" diverts flow away from stenosed segments.
  • Wall motion localization: apical → LAD; inferior → RCA; lateral → LCx.

RV infarction unique profile

  • Inferior STEMI + JVD + clear lungs + hypotension = RV involvement.
  • Cath: ↑ CVP, ↓ PCWP, ↓ CO, ↑ SVR. The ↑ CVP + ↓ PCWP combination is pathognomonic.
  • V4R lead ST↑ confirms.
  • Why RV recovers: low O₂ extraction reserve, perfused both systole + diastole, more collaterals.
  • Treatment: IV FLUIDS (preload-dependent).
  • AVOID nitroglycerin, diuretics, morphine (all drop preload → cardiogenic shock).
  • Emergent PCI of RCA.

Ischemia biochemistry cell death timeline

  • < 60 s: aerobic → anaerobic; lactate ↑; contraction stops.
  • 1–20 min: cell swelling from ↑ intracellular Na⁺ + Ca²⁺ (osmotic).
  • 20–30 min: irreversible threshold — mitochondrial permeability transition + severe membrane damage.
  • EM marker of irreversibility: mitochondrial vacuolization with flocculent densities.
  • > 30 min: coagulative necrosis; troponin leaks (rises ~3 h, peaks 24 h).

Stunned vs hibernating vs scarred recoverability

  • Stunned: brief acute ischemia + reperfusion → transient dysfunction → spontaneously reversible.
  • Hibernating: chronic underperfusion → adaptive ↓ contractility/metabolism → reversible with revascularization.
  • Scarred: post-MI fibrosis (TGF-β driven) → irreversible.
  • Viability testing (PET, dobutamine echo, cardiac MRI delayed gadolinium) distinguishes hibernating from scarred → guides revascularization decision.

Special angina syndromes Prinzmetal + cocaine

  • Vasospastic (Prinzmetal) angina: young woman, rest pain at night, transient ST↑, no fixed CAD on cath. Migraine + Raynaud comorbidities. Treatment: CCB + nitrates. AVOID β-blockers, sumatriptan, ergots.
  • Cocaine-induced: young patient, sympathomimetic (dilated pupils, HTN, tachy, sweat). α1 vasoconstriction + β1 demand. Treatment: benzodiazepines + nitroglycerin. AVOID pure β-blockers (unopposed α). Phentolamine or labetalol acceptable.

Embolic + watershed cross-references

  • Atheroembolism post cath: needle-shaped cholesterol clefts + multinucleated giant cells. Blue toe, livedo, Hollenhorst plaques (retina), AKI with eosinophilia.
  • Renal infarction: wedge lesion + flank pain + ↑ LDH; cardioembolic from AFib (LA appendage thrombus).
  • Watershed colonic ischemia: splenic flexure (SMA-IMA border) after hypotensive event.
  • Watershed brain: parasagittal cortex → "man-in-the-barrel" (proximal arm weakness).
  • CA1 hippocampus + Purkinje cells = selectively vulnerable to global hypoperfusion.

Sudden cardiac death post-MI arrhythmic + mechanical

  • ~50% of MI deaths occur out-of-hospital in the first hour — usually VF.
  • Ischemic AP shortens unevenly → reentrant circuits → VT → VF.
  • Days 5–14: free wall rupture / papillary rupture / IVS rupture.
  • Long-term SCD prevention: ICD if EF < 35% post-MI; β-blockers reduce arrhythmic death.

Top 15 traps

  1. Plaque rupture risk = STABILITY, not stenosis size.
  2. Internal mammary arteries resist atherosclerosis → LIMA = CABG conduit.
  3. Atherogenesis first step = endothelial injury.
  4. Fibrous cap built by smooth muscle cells; lipid core built by foam cells.
  5. Cap rupture mediated by macrophage-released MMPs.
  6. Stable angina symptom threshold ≈ 70% stenosis.
  7. STEMI = transmural; NSTEMI = subendocardial; both = troponin positive.
  8. RV infarction cath: ↑ CVP + ↓ PCWP — pathognomonic combo.
  9. NEVER give NTG in RV infarction; give IV fluids.
  10. Papillary rupture 3–5 d (RCA, posteromedial single supply); free wall rupture 5–14 d (LAD).
  11. Light microscopy normal until 4+ h post-MI; troponin rises ~3 h.
  12. Stunned: brief, spontaneously recovers. Hibernating: chronic, recovers with revascularization. Scarred: permanent.
  13. Mitochondrial vacuolization with flocculent densities = irreversibility marker.
  14. TGF-β drives post-MI scar collagen deposition.
  15. Cocaine MI: benzos + NTG; avoid β-blockers. Prinzmetal: CCB first-line, avoid β-blockers.
