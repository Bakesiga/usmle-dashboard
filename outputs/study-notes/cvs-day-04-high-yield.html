Hypertension primary vs secondary

  • Primary (~90%): no identifiable cause; age, obesity, salt, family history.
  • Secondary (~10%): search list when young / refractory / abrupt onset / disproportionate end-organ damage.
  • Renal parenchymal disease (CKD) — most common secondary.
  • Renovascular (RAS): atherosclerotic plaque at renal artery origin (older); FMD "string-of-beads" (younger women).
  • 1° aldosteronism (Conn): HTN + ↓ K + ↓ renin + ↑ aldo.
  • Pheochromocytoma: paroxysmal HTN + headache + sweating + palpitations.
  • Cushing, hyperthyroid, coarctation, OSA, NSAIDs/OCP.

RAS clinical anchors renovascular HTN

  • Refractory HTN, flash pulmonary edema, abdominal bruit.
  • AKI after starting ACE-i (efferent constriction was holding GFR up).
  • Atrophic stenotic kidney (cortical thinning, tubular atrophy, crowded glomeruli).
  • Contralateral kidney develops hyaline arteriolosclerosis from systemic HTN exposure.
  • Imaging: Doppler US; CTA/MRA confirm.

HTN emergency end-organ damage

  • BP > 180/120 + end-organ damage (encephalopathy, ICH, ACS, pulm edema, AKI, MAHA, papilledema).
  • Malignant nephrosclerosis: fibrinoid necrosis + onion-skin + MAHA (schistocytes).
  • Lower MAP ≤ 20–25% in first hour — autoregulation right-shifted; rapid drop = ATN.
  • Flat PRA on ARB+thiazide = nonadherence (renin should surge).
  • Concentric LVH from chronic HTN: ↑ mass, small cavity, ↑ angiotensin II local trophic signal, β-MHC upregulation.
  • Endpoint: HFpEF → AFib → flash pulmonary edema.

AAA infrarenal · MMP-driven

  • Focal dilation > 50% normal (or > 3 cm abdominal aorta).
  • Infrarenal; tenuous vasa vasorum + chronic inflammation → MMPs degrade elastin/collagen.
  • Risks: age > 60, male, smoking (strongest), HTN, family hx. DM is mildly protective.
  • Screening: one-time abdominal US for men 65–75 ever-smokers.
  • Repair: > 5.5 cm, expanding > 0.5 cm/6 mo, symptomatic, ruptured.
  • Ruptured AAA: sudden abdo/back pain + syncope + hypotension + pulsatile mass.
  • Posterior rupture → Grey-Turner (flank) or Cullen (periumbilical) ecchymosis.

Thoracic aortic aneurysm cystic medial degeneration

  • Ascending aorta predominantly.
  • CMD histology: elastic lamellae fragmentation + mucopolysaccharide pools in media.
  • Risks: chronic HTN (aging CMD); Marfan, Loeys-Dietz, EDS vascular; bicuspid AV; syphilitic aortitis.
  • Compression triad: cough + dysphagia + hoarseness (trachea, esophagus, recurrent laryngeal nerve).
  • CXR: wide mediastinum, prominent knob.
  • Lathyrism / BAPN inhibits lysyl oxidase → defective collagen/elastin cross-links → CMD.

Aortic dissection intimal tear + false lumen

  • Intimal tear → blood enters media → false lumen propagates.
  • Strongest risk: chronic HTN; CMD substrate (Marfan etc.).
  • Sudden tearing chest/back pain; BP discrepancy > 20 mmHg between arms; widened mediastinum on CXR.
  • Stanford A (ascending) → surgical emergency. Stanford B (distal to L subclavian) → medical (BP control).
  • Complications cascade: tamponade (pericardial rupture); acute AR (root extension); MI (coronary ostium); stroke (carotid); limb/renal/mesenteric ischemia.
  • Treatment ordering: β-blocker (esmolol) FIRST to reduce dP/dt → then add nitroprusside. Never start vasodilator alone.

Atherosclerosis + PAD intimal lipid plaques

  • Atherogenesis: endothelial injury → LDL → oxidation → foam cells (macrophages) → fatty streak → SMC migration (PDGF from platelets) → fibrous plaque → complicated.
  • Burden: abdominal aorta > coronaries > popliteal > carotid > circle of Willis.
  • Internal mammary arteries protected → CABG conduit of choice.
  • Leriche syndrome: aortoiliac occlusion → bilateral claudication + impotence + diminished femoral pulses.
  • Acute limb ischemia 6 Ps: Pain, Pallor, Poikilothermia, Paresthesia, Paralysis, Pulselessness.
  • Embolic sources: AFib (LA appendage), LV thrombus, IE vegetations, atrial myxoma (gelatinous mass; LA fossa ovalis area).
  • Reperfusion injury: ROS + mito permeability + neutrophils + complement → CK surge.

Venous disease valves + hemosiderin

  • Varicose veins: incompetent valves → reflux → dilation. Risks: standing job, pregnancy, obesity.
  • Chronic venous insufficiency: stasis dermatitis, lipodermatosclerosis ("inverted champagne bottle"), hemosiderin pigmentation.
  • Bronze-brown skin = hemosiderin from RBC extravasation across leaky capillaries.
  • Stasis ulcers at medial malleolus (arterial ulcers at lateral malleolus/toes).

Coarctation cross-ref Day 10 preview

  • Narrowing near ligamentum arteriosum.
  • Upper-body HTN + brachial-femoral delay; rib notching on CXR.
  • Associations: Turner syndrome, bicuspid AV, berry aneurysm → SAH.
  • Neonate critical coarct: shock when ductus closes; PGE1 keeps it open.

Top 15 traps

  1. Malignant HTN renal histology: fibrinoid necrosis + onion-skin + MAHA.
  2. Lower BP gradually in HTN emergency — autoregulation right-shifted.
  3. Flat PRA on ARB+thiazide = nonadherence.
  4. Diabetes is mildly protective against AAA.
  5. Internal mammary arteries are atherosclerosis-resistant → LIMA = CABG conduit.
  6. Marfan: ectopia lentis upward; homocystinuria: downward.
  7. Lathyrism / Menkes / EDS kyphoscoliotic = lysyl oxidase defect → CMD.
  8. Dissection treatment: β-blocker FIRST, then vasodilator.
  9. PAN is ANCA-negative + spares lungs.
  10. Buerger involves veins + nerves; treatment = smoking cessation only.
  11. c-ANCA → GPA (airway + granulomas + renal); p-ANCA → MPA or EGPA.
  12. Carotid sinus → CN IX; aortic arch → CN X.
  13. L gonadal → L renal → IVC; nutcracker syndrome at L renal.
  14. Right adult varicocele = RCC invading IVC.
  15. Subclavian steal: retrograde vertebral flow + interarm SBP > 15 mmHg difference.
