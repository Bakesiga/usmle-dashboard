PV loops, valves & sounds · Print and stick on your wall

Core equations memorise cold

  • CO = SV × HR.
  • SV = EDV − ESV.
  • EF = SV / EDV; normal ≥ 50%; <40% = HFrEF.
  • Fick: CO = VO₂ / (CaO₂ − CvO₂).
  • MAP ≈ DBP + 1/3 PP ≈ CO × SVR.
  • Pulse pressure ↑ with ↑ SV or ↓ aortic compliance.
  • Poiseuille: R ∝ ηL / r⁴ → radius dominates.

Preload · afterload · contractility drivers of SV

  • Preload = EDV / EDP; depends on venous return.
  • Afterload ≈ aortic DBP; LV must exceed to open AV.
  • Contractility = intrinsic force at a given EDV (independent of preload).
  • Frank-Starling: ↑ EDV → ↑ sarcomere length → ↑ SV (within range).
  • ↑ contractility: catecholamines, digoxin, ↑ Ca²⁺, ↓ extracellular Na⁺.
  • ↓ contractility: β-blockers, non-DHP CCBs, acidosis, hypoxia.

PV loop — landmarks & shifts draw it every day

  • Points: 1 = MV closes (S1) · 2 = AV opens · 3 = AV closes (S2) · 4 = MV opens.
  • Width = SV. Steeper ESPVR (top-left line) = ↑ contractility.
  • ↑ Preload (saline): shifts right, wider, ↑ EDV, ↑ SV.
  • ↑ Afterload (phenylephrine): taller, ↑ ESV, ↓ SV.
  • ↑ Contractility (dobutamine): steeper ESPVR → ↓ ESV, ↑ SV.
  • Nitroglycerin (venodilator): narrower, ↓ EDV, ↓ EDSL, ↓ SV.
  • Nitroprusside (balanced): smaller at both ends, SV preserved.
  • AV fistula: ↑ EDV + ↓ afterload → wide loop, ↑ SV (chronic = high-output HF).
  • AS: peak LV >> peak aortic; tall loop with persistent gradient.
  • AR: wide loop, low DBP, ↑ EDV (regurg); eccentric LVH.
  • MS: LV loop normal/small (LV under-filled); LA hypertension carries the disease. Opening snap at point 4.
  • MR: no isovolumetric contraction phase; regurg starts at S1.

Function curves cardiac × vascular

  • Cardiac fn curve: ↑ CVP → ↑ CO; shifts up with ↑ contractility.
  • Vascular fn curve: ↑ CO → ↓ CVP; shifts right with ↑ volume.
  • Intersection = operating point.
  • Hemorrhage → vascular curve left → ↓ CO + ↓ CVP.
  • Exercise → cardiac up + vascular right (venoconstriction).
  • AV fistula → vascular right + cardiac up → ↑ CO, mild ↑ CVP.
  • HFrEF → cardiac curve flattens → ↓ CO + ↑ CVP.

Wiggers · JVP timing

  • S1 = MV/TV closure (start of systole).
  • S2 = AV/PV closure (start of diastole).
  • S3 = early diastolic; rapid filling against dilated/stiff LV.
  • S4 = late diastolic; atrial kick into stiff LV.
  • JVP: a = atrial contraction (gone in AF) · c = TV bulge · x = atrial relax · v = atrial fill (giant in TR) · y = TV opens.
  • Cannon a waves: complete AV block (atria fire against closed TV).

S2 splitting recognise 4 patterns

  • Physiologic: inspiration splits A2–P2; expiration unifies.
  • Wide: delayed P2 — RBBB, PS, severe MR.
  • Fixed: ASD (constant L→R equalises RA pressures).
  • Paradoxical: A2 after P2 — LBBB, severe AS, HCM. Splits on expiration.

Exercise · aging hemodynamics

  • Aerobic exercise: ↑ HR, ↑ SV, ↑ CO; muscle vasodilation > splanchnic vasoconstriction → ↓ SVR.
  • Net BP: SBP ↑, DBP unchanged, PP ↑.
  • Pre-loaded by venoconstriction (capacitance ↓).
  • Aging aorta: ↓ compliance → ISH (high SBP, normal DBP, wide PP).
  • Aged myocyte: hypertrophy without hyperplasia; lipofuscin pigment (wear-and-tear).
  • Hypovolemic shock fluids: ↑ EDV → ↑ EDSL → ↑ SV (Frank-Starling).

Top 15 traps

  1. EF formula = SV/EDV (not SV/ESV).
  2. HCM murmur SOFTER with handgrip — opposite of AS.
  3. Fixed split S2 = ASD (the RA pressures are tied to LA across the shunt).
  4. Paradoxical split = LBBB or severe AS (split on expiration).
  5. MS leaves LV hemodynamics normal — only LA hypertension.
  6. Cath profile for MS: ↑ PCWP with normal LV.
  7. Opening snap is at point 4 on the PV loop (MV opens).
  8. AR signs: wide PP + water-hammer + de Musset + Duroziez + Quincke + Hill + Müller + Traube.
  9. TR / right-sided murmurs louder with inspiration (Carvallo sign).
  10. Posteromedial papillary rupture 3–5 d post-MI → acute MR.
  11. Nitroglycerin = venous only (↓ preload). Nitroprusside = balanced (SV preserved).
  12. S3 = early diastole / rapid filling; S4 = late diastole / atrial kick.
  13. Eccentric LVH = volume; concentric LVH = pressure.
  14. Aging aorta → ISH; pulse pressure widens.
  15. Cannon a waves = complete AV block (atria firing against closed TV).
