Day 20 lecture summary · First Aid 2025 pp. 704 to 707

1 · Asthma and COPD Drugs FA pp. 704 to 705

  • Bronchoconstriction is driven by inflammation and parasympathetic (vagal) tone, so therapy targets both.
  • Beta-2 agonists: short-acting albuterol relaxes airway smooth muscle for acute attacks. Long-acting salmeterol and formoterol are for maintenance and should not be used as monotherapy in asthma.
  • Muscarinic antagonists: ipratropium (short) and tiotropium (long) block M3 receptors to bronchodilate. Especially useful in COPD.
  • Inhaled corticosteroids: fluticasone and budesonide are the first-line controllers for persistent asthma (anti-inflammatory).
  • Antileukotrienes: montelukast and zafirlukast block the CysLT1 receptor and are especially good for aspirin-exacerbated and exercise-induced asthma. Zileuton inhibits 5-lipoxygenase and can be hepatotoxic.
  • Anti-IgE: omalizumab, a monoclonal antibody against IgE, for severe allergic asthma. Anti-IL-5 agents (mepolizumab) target eosinophilic asthma.
  • Methylxanthines: theophylline inhibits phosphodiesterase to raise cAMP, but has a narrow therapeutic window with cardiac and neurotoxicity, and is metabolized by cytochrome P-450.

2 · Pulmonary Hypertension Drugs FA p. 706

  • Endothelin receptor antagonists: bosentan blocks endothelin-1 (a vasoconstrictor); hepatotoxic.
  • PDE-5 inhibitors: sildenafil raises cGMP to dilate pulmonary vessels.
  • Prostacyclin analogs: epoprostenol and iloprost mimic PGI2 for vasodilation.
  • Guanylate cyclase stimulator: riociguat.

3 · Cough, Congestion, Secretions FA p. 707

  • Dextromethorphan: a cough suppressant and mild NMDA antagonist.
  • Decongestants: pseudoephedrine and phenylephrine are alpha agonists that reduce nasal swelling.
  • Guaifenesin: an expectorant that thins secretions.
  • N-acetylcysteine: a mucolytic that loosens mucus, and the antidote for acetaminophen overdose.

Key rules

  1. Albuterol for acute, inhaled steroids for control.
  2. Never a long-acting beta-agonist alone in asthma.
  3. Montelukast for aspirin and exercise asthma.
  4. Bosentan blocks endothelin.
  5. Sildenafil raises cGMP.
