Action potentials → ECG → shock physiology · Print and stick on your wall

Ventricular AP — 5 phases non-pacemaker

  • Phase 0: rapid depol, Na⁺ influx.
  • Phase 1: initial repol, Na⁺ inactivates, transient K⁺ out.
  • Phase 2: plateau, L-type Ca²⁺ influx balances K⁺ efflux (unique to cardiac).
  • Phase 3: rapid repol, K⁺ efflux dominates.
  • Phase 4: resting at −90 mV via K⁺ leak.
  • ERP = phases 0–3 (Na⁺ channels inactivated).

Pacemaker AP — SA, AV slow-response

  • No stable resting potential.
  • Phase 4 (spontaneous depol): HCN "funny" Na⁺ → T-type Ca²⁺ → L-type Ca²⁺ approaching threshold.
  • Phase 0: L-type Ca²⁺ slow upstroke.
  • Phase 3: K⁺ efflux repol.
  • Drug targets: β-blocker (↓ HCN slope); non-DHP CCB (L-type Ca block); adenosine (↑ K⁺ → hyperpol); ivabradine (HCN only).

EC coupling · NCX · digoxin how Ca couples

  • AP → L-type Ca²⁺ opens (phase 2) → small Ca²⁺ in.
  • Trigger Ca²⁺ → Ca²⁺-induced Ca²⁺ release from SR (ryanodine receptor).
  • Ca²⁺ binds troponin C → tropomyosin moves → actin-myosin cross-bridge cycling.
  • Relaxation: SERCA pumps Ca²⁺ back into SR; NCX extrudes Ca²⁺ using Na⁺ gradient.
  • Digoxin: blocks Na/K ATPase → ↑ Na⁺ in → NCX runs less → ↑ Ca²⁺ in → ↑ contractility.

Conduction system velocity + escape rates

  • SA → atria → AV (delay ~100 ms) → His → bundle branches → Purkinje → ventricles.
  • Velocity mnemonic — Park At Ventura, AV: Purkinje > atria > ventricles > AV node.
  • Pacemaker rates: SA 60–100 · AV/junctional 40–60 (narrow QRS escape) · Purkinje 20–40 (wide QRS escape).
  • AV node refractoriness = the rate-limiting step in AFib, atrial flutter, PSVT.

ECG read in 4 steps rate · rhythm · axis · intervals

  • Rate: 300 / big boxes; or R in 6 s × 10.
  • Rhythm: sinus = P before every QRS, upright in II.
  • Axis: I↑ + aVF↓ = LAD; I↓ + aVF↑ = RAD.
  • Intervals: PR 0.12–0.20; QRS <0.12; QTc <0.44.
  • Chamber: LAE = wide-notched P (p-mitrale); RAE = tall P (p-pulmonale).
  • Blocks: 1° = long PR · Mobitz I = progressive PR → drop · Mobitz II = constant PR + drops · 3° = AV dissociation.

Ablation map anatomic targets

  • AFib → pulmonary vein isolation.
  • Atrial flutter → cavotricuspid isthmus.
  • AVNRT → slow pathway of AV node.
  • WPW → accessory pathway (bundle of Kent).
  • RVOT VT → right ventricular outflow tract.

Coronary autoregulation + NO why diastole matters

  • Heart extracts > 70% O₂ at rest → ↑ demand met by ↑ flow.
  • Autoregulation over MAP 60–140 mmHg via adenosine + NO.
  • NO: eNOS uses L-arginine → NO → smooth muscle GC → ↑ cGMP → vasodilation.
  • LV perfused mostly in diastole; subendocardium most vulnerable to ischemia.
  • Coronary sinus = lowest O₂ saturation in the body.

Baroreceptors · chemoreceptors CN IX vs X

  • Carotid sinus baro → CN IX.
  • Aortic arch baro → CN X.
  • Same for chemoreceptors: carotid body → IX, aortic body → X.
  • ↑ BP → ↑ firing → ↑ vagal + ↓ sympathetic → ↓ BP.
  • ↓ BP → ↓ firing → ↓ vagal + ↑ sympathetic → ↑ HR, vasoconstriction.
  • Carotid sinus hypersensitivity (tight collar) → exaggerated reflex syncope.
  • Vasovagal syncope: cardioinhibitory (↑ vagal) + vasodepressor (↓ sympathetic) limbs fire together.

Heart failure cascade + pharmacology block bad arms, augment good

  • HFrEF activates maladaptive neurohormonal cascade: ↑ sympathetic, ↑ RAAS, ↑ ADH; ↑ ANP/BNP is the only counter-regulatory arm.
  • ANP from atria, BNP from ventricles, in response to myocardial stretch → guanylate cyclase → ↑ cGMP → vasodilation, natriuresis, ↓ aldosterone, ↓ renin.
  • Four-pillar HFrEF regimen: β-blocker (carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol) + ACE-i / ARB / ARNi (sacubitril/valsartan blocks neprilysin → ↑ ANP/BNP) + MRA (spironolactone, eplerenone) + SGLT2-i (dapa, empa).
  • Diuretic = symptom only, no mortality benefit.
  • Acute decompensated HF with HTN: nitroprusside (balanced; preload + afterload ↓, SV preserved).

Top 15 traps

  1. Ventricular phase 0 = Na⁺; pacemaker phase 0 = Ca²⁺.
  2. HCN "funny" current drives pacemaker phase 4 spontaneous depol.
  3. Conduction speed: Park-At-Ventura, AV (Purkinje fastest, AV slowest).
  4. Carotid → IX; aortic → X for both baro and chemoreceptors.
  5. AFib triggers: pulmonary veins. Atrial flutter: cavotricuspid isthmus.
  6. Vagal maneuvers and adenosine break AVNRT by prolonging AV refractoriness.
  7. Coronary sinus = lowest O₂ in body; LV perfused in diastole.
  8. NO substrate = L-arginine; mediator = cGMP.
  9. Digoxin: ↑ Na⁺ inside → NCX runs less → ↑ Ca²⁺ → ↑ contractility.
  10. Cardiogenic shock SVR is UP (compensatory), not down.
  11. Distributive shock has high CO + low SVR (except neurogenic).
  12. Tamponade PCWP appears ↑ (external compression — LA pressure transmitted).
  13. Sacubitril blocks neprilysin → ↑ ANP/BNP. Valsartan blocks AT1.
  14. Complete AV block: narrow QRS escape from His (better); wide from Purkinje (worse).
  15. Vasovagal syncope = cardioinhibitory + vasodepressor combined misfire.
