About Allan

Allan finished medical school at Makerere University, completed a Master's in Global Health at Duke, and is starting PGY-1 Neurology training at Creighton University this summer. While at Duke he served as a teaching assistant for Epidemiology & Biostatistics, and has spent the last two years leading daily USMLE study groups. Sample June plan — Step 1

Every chapter is mapped page-by-page to First Aid 2025, broken into daily blocks with practice questions. Here's how the first month looks:

Cardiovascular 10 days · Jun 1–10
  • Day 1 — Orientation, embryology, and congenital heart defects
  • Day 2 — Cardiac anatomy and imaging
  • Day 3 — Cardiac output and pressure-volume loops
  • Day 4 — Heart sounds and murmurs
  • Day 5 — ECG, action potentials, and BP control
  • Day 6 — Coronary artery disease, IHD, and myocardial infarction
  • Day 7 — Hypertension and atherosclerosis
  • Day 8 — Heart failure and cardiomyopathies
  • Day 9 — Endocarditis, myocarditis, and vasculitis
  • Day 10 — Cardiovascular pharmacology
Respiratory 8 days · Jun 11–18
  • Day 1 — Embryology, surfactant, and congenital anomalies
  • Day 2 — Anatomy and muscles of breathing
  • Day 3 — Lung volumes and ventilation mechanics
  • Day 4 — Gas exchange and V/Q mismatch
  • Day 5 — Control of breathing and sleep apnea
  • Day 6 — Asthma and COPD
  • Day 7 — Restrictive disease, ARDS, and pneumonia
  • Day 8 — TB, lung cancer, and respiratory pharmacology
Epi & Biostats 4 days · Jun 19–22
  • Day 1 — Study designs, levels of evidence, and diagnostic testing
  • Day 2 — Quantifying risk, demographic transitions, and survival analysis
  • Day 3 — Bias, confounding, and effect modification
  • Day 4 — Statistical distributions, statistical testing, and confidence intervals
General Pathology 8 days · Jun 23–30
  • Day 1 — Cellular adaptations and apoptosis
  • Day 2 — Necrosis, ischemia, and free radicals
  • Day 3 — Acute inflammation
  • Day 4 — Chronic inflammation and tissue repair
  • Day 5 — Neoplasia I — hallmarks and metastases
  • Day 6 — Neoplasia II — oncogenes and tumor suppressors
  • Day 7 — Neoplasia III — tumor markers and aging
  • Day 8 — General pathology review

Endocrine, GI, Heme/Onc, MSK, Neuro, Psych, Renal, Reproductive, and Immunology blocks follow July onward. The full month-by-month schedule is visible on the student dashboard once enrolled.

WHY THIS COHORT WORKS

Reading First Aid isn't the same as passing Step 1.

The exam tests how you apply facts under a clinical vignette. So every session ends inside UWorld, working through that day's questions together — ruling options in and out, surfacing the test-writer's logic. By exam day you've seen the patterns, not just the pages.
