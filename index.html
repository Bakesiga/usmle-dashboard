About Allan

Allan finished medical school at Makerere University, completed a Master's in Global Health at Duke, and is starting PGY-1 Neurology training at Creighton University this summer. While at Duke he served as a teaching assistant for Epidemiology & Biostatistics, and has spent the last two years leading daily USMLE study groups. He has walked the IMG path himself — from ECFMG application through the residency match — and built this cohort around the lessons he wished someone had handed him at the start.

Sample June plan — Step 1

Every chapter is mapped page-by-page to First Aid 2025, broken into daily blocks with practice questions. Here's how the first month looks:

Cardiovascular 12 days · Jun 1–12
  • Day 1 — Orientation & overview
  • Day 2 — Embryology, fetal circulation, congenital heart defects
  • Day 3 — Anatomy: chambers, CT/CXR, pericardium
  • Day 4 — Pump physiology, PV loops, cardiac cycle
  • Day 5 — Heart sounds, splitting, murmurs & maneuvers
  • Day 6 — Action potentials, ECG, BP control
  • Day 7 — CAD, IHD, MI evolution & complications
  • Day 8 — HTN, hyperlipidemia, atherosclerosis, aneurysms
  • Day 9 — Cardiomyopathies, HF, shock
  • Day 10 — Rheumatic, IE, myocarditis, vasculitis
  • Day 11 — Cardiovascular pharmacology
  • Day 12 — Epi/Biostats applied to CV (trials, sens/spec)
Respiratory 10 days · Jun 13–22
  • Day 1 — Embryology, surfactant, congenital anomalies
  • Day 2 — Anatomy: respiratory tree, segments, diaphragm
  • Day 3 — Lung volumes, mechanics, O₂–Hb dissociation
  • Day 4 — A–a gradient, V/Q, pulmonary circulation
  • Day 5 — Control of breathing, sleep apnea
  • Day 6 — Upper airway + obstructive lung disease
  • Day 7 — Restrictive disease, ARDS, vascular, pneumonia
  • Day 8 — TB, pleural disease, lung cancer, pharm
  • Day 9 — Epi/Biostats applied to Respiratory
  • Day 10 — Respiratory chapter review & practice
General Pathology 8 days · Jun 23–30
  • Day 1 — Cellular adaptations, reversible injury, apoptosis
  • Day 2 — Necrosis, ischemia, free radicals
  • Day 3 — Acute inflammation
  • Day 4 — Chronic inflammation & tissue repair
  • Day 5 — Neoplasia I: hallmarks, grade/stage, metastases
  • Day 6 — Neoplasia II: oncogenes, TSGs, paraneoplastic
  • Day 7 — Neoplasia III + Aging
  • Day 8 — General pathology review & practice

Endocrine, GI, Heme/Onc, MSK, Neuro, Psych, Renal, Reproductive, and immunology blocks follow July onward — full schedule available on the student dashboard once enrolled.
